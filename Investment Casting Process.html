Vasuki's Investment Casting Process

Pattern Creation:

" FINAL CASTING QUALITY DEPENDS SOLELY ON THE WAX PATTERN QUALITY "
  • A wax model, also known as a pattern, is created in the shape of the desired part. This can be done by injecting molten wax into a metal mold, We have a Wax pattern shop equipped with automatic and manual wax injection machines. We have state of the art combination of modern machinery and skilled hands to achieve the highest possible quality of wax patterns.

Pattern Assembly:

  • Before assembly, each and every pattern is going through strict visual inspection and then the wax patterns are attached to a central wax sprue, forming a tree-like structure. The sprue serves as a guide channel for the molten metal to flow into the patterns while pouring.

Shell Building & drying:

  • The wax assembly is dipped into a liquid ceramic slurry, coating the entire surface, After the slurry dries, the assembly is covered with fine ceramic sand for that we use ZIRCON FLOUR, FINE SAND, FUSED SILICA & COLLOIDAL SILICA (water-based). and this process is repeated several times, with progressively coarser layers of ceramic and bonding material, to build a strong and durable shell around the wax patterns.
  • We have temperature and humidity-controlled drying rooms equipped with dehumidifiers to completely dry out shells to achieve desired moisture level before the dewaxing process.

Dewaxing:

  • The ceramic shell is placed in a furnace to autoclave, where the wax melts and drains out, leaving a hollow cavity in the shape of the desired part. The wax can be collected and reused for future patterns. For this process, we have the industry standard automatic dewaxing plant.

Shell Firing:

  • The ceramic shell is fired at high temperatures in this process to remove any remaining wax residue and to harden and strengthen the shell. This step also helps to eliminate any moisture and impurities from the shell.

Melting & Pouring:

  • Molten metal is poured into the preheated ceramic shell. The metal flows through the sprue and into the cavities left by the wax patterns, filling all the intricate details of the part. Our melting shop is equipped with INDUCTOTHERM to make induction furnaces of 150KV, 300KV Crucibles.

Shell Removal:

  • Once the metal has fully solidified, the ceramic shell is removed, typically by using vibratory equipment. Any remaining shell material can be removed by sandblasting or other in-house cleaning processes.

Gate Cutting & Grinding:

  • The individual metal parts are cut from the sprue using a gas-cutting or saw-cutting method. The remaining sprue material and any imperfections are ground down to achieve the desired surface finish and dimensional accuracy.

Fettling and finishing :

  • Skilled hands make sure to remove any remaining shell material and imperfections to achieve the desired surface finish with clean and clear casting.

Heat treatment:

  • In-house facility for heat treatment like; Solution Annealing, Normalizing, Tempering, with temperature controlled Gas fired Heat Treatment Furnace.
  • Best Heat treatment practice is ensured through study of microstructure.

Finishing & Inspection:

  • Each and every lot is inspected for dimensional accuracy, surface finish, and material quality by visual inspection, before packaging and dispatch.

Machining:

  • If required we are doing machining as per customer drawing and desired accuracy, We have collaborated with our machining vendor for VMC, CNC & HMC machining jobs.
